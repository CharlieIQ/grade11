Upbringing

Ernesto Guevara de la Serna was born on June 14th 1928. He was the oldest of 5 children. He suffered from asthma, even though he was a great athlete and student. He spent his holidays observing the poverty in Latin America.

His journey

In December 1951 he took a 9 month trip around latin america while on break from medical school with his friend Alberto Granado. Their journey took them from Argentina through Chile, Peru, Colombia, and to Venezuela, from which Guevara traveled alone to Miami, and returned to Argentina by plane. During the trip Guevara kept a journal that was published under the name: The Motorcycle Diaries: Notes on a Latin American Journey. His trip was the cataclyst that would cause him to dedicate his life to helping the poor and unfortunate.

The Cuban Revolution

It was in Mexico that Che met two brothers named Raul and Fidel Castro. They wanted to overthrow the dictator in Cuba, Fulgencie Batista who ran an oppressive regime. On December 2nd 1956, he landed on cuba with a company of 86 men. They were detected almost instantly, and were decimated by Batista's army. A wounded Che Guevara with the survivors of his guerillas, joined a larger guerilla movement in Sierra Mastra. Che became the medic and helped the wounded guerillas as the movement increased in size.

The Fall of the Batiste Regime

The revolutionary movement increased in size and numbers, getting more weapons and recruits as Fidel's troops swept across the country. They entered Havana (The capital city of Cuba) in January 8th 1959. Guevara served at the main prison in Havana where he oversaw the punishment of counter revolutionaries.

How he helped his community

He helped farmers in Cuba make the most of their land by taking power away from monopolizing land owners and giving power back to peasants. During the Batiste regime, the Cuban literacy rate was 60-70% across the country. Che helped the government build schools in rural areas in the hopes that the literacy rate would climb for peasants. This in turn increased the literacy rate to 96%. He became so influental in helping the people of Cuba, that he was invited to speak at the UN in New York City. At the UN he spoke against apartheid (segregation) in South Africa and condemned the US for meddling in foreign policy. He tried to lead by example, working tirelessly at his job and doing volunteer working to encourage the Cuban people to work to their potenital.

The fall of Che Guevara

While trying to spread his revolutionary ideas to nearby Bolivia in October of 1967, the United States began to catch wind of it. In this time, the United States was engaged in an economic war with the Soviet Union (Present day Russia) and the US, a capitalist society, was trying to stamp out communism in nearby regions. Cuba, being a communist society was in the US's radar. On October 8th, a CIA backed Bolivian force captured Che Guevara and his Bolivian company. He was interrogated and executed by a Bolivian soldier who has lost his friends in the previous battle with Guevaras guerillas. He is quoted saying "Wherever death may surprise us, let it be welcome, provided that this our battle cry may have reached some receptive ear and another hand may be extended to wield our weapons"
